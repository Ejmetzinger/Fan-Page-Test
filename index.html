Types of Blades

There are many types of knives and along with them their blade different blade types, this page will be dedicated to the blades and another to some popular types of knives.

Drop Point

The first and most common type of blade is called the Drop Point, it is curved and it is one of the most used blade types for knives.

Clip Point

Another common blade type is the Clip Point, this knife looks like a typical bowie knife and the tip of the blade is "Clipped" off to give it's distinct shape.

Spear Point

There is a blade that looks like a spear head, these blades are mostly known for being able to puncture and slice with ease.

Sheep Foot

Sheep foot blades are great for cutting through tough materials and are used mainly by first responders and sailors for tough situations.

Tanto

The Tanto blade is styled from after a traditional Japanese balde, they were made popular because of their versatility.Many military and Police groups have adopted this blade style.

Blunt Tip

These are more like modifications to an existing blade than an actual blade type. They are mainly used by kayaking,water sports,scuba-divers,and fishermen their blades are not pointed at the the tip so they cannot puncture through their boat or oxygen line. They are also highly water resistant and can resist corrosion.

Pocket Knife

One of the most puoular and best known knives, these are known for their versitility and all of the tools that they come with they are mostly used in camping situations and are carried by lots of people everyday.

Trailing Point Blade

Almost like a Drop Point but curved backwards, these blades are best for slicing and cutting that are commonly used by hunters and fisher men to cut and clean their catch.

Straight Back Blade

These are blades with a straight back and a curved blade, they are used in everything from kitchen knives to survival blades they are what most people are looking for our of a nife and that is what makes them a popular choice.

Needle Point Blade

These blades are mainly used in fighting or self defense blades, like the Spear Point they taper down but at a greater angle than a Spear point.

Spey Point Blades

These blades were traditionally used for spaying animals and other surgical or removal processes, they have one straight back anc a curved blade, they are mostly used for skinning purposes in modern times.

Warncliffe Blade.

These blades are similar to the SheepFoot blade in design but the blade is much thicker.

Serrated Blade

These blades along with a sharpened edge have a serrated edge, these can be useful while in the wilderness or for first responders.
